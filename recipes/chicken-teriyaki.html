Teriyaki Chicken

Description

I have enjoyed teriyaki many times at Japanese restaurants. If I've made it at home I've probably just bought a bottle of teriyaki sauce and brushed it on some chicken. This is an authentic verison. I'm very glad I did it. The term teriyaki comes from two Japanese words teri and yaki. Teri refers to the shine or luster of the glazed sauce, and yaki means to broil or grill the meat.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 1 cup sake
  • 1/2 cup mirin (Japanese sweet wine)
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 3 green onions, chopped, white and dark green parts seperated
  • 2 teaspoons finely grated ginger
  • 10 skinless, boneless chicken thighs
  • 1 teaspoon vegetable oil, or as needed

Method

Step 1

Whisk soy sauce, sake, mirin, brown sugar, white parts of green onions, and ginger together in a saucepan; bring to a simmer and cook until sugar dissolves and marinade for approx 5 minutes. Pour marinade into a bowl and cool to room temperature.

Step 2

Pour 1/2 the marinade into a resealable plastic bag, Add chicken thighs, coat with the marinade, squeeze out excess air, and seal the bag, Marinate into the refrigerator for about 30 minutes to an 1 hour.

Step 3

Set oven rack about 6 inches from the heat source and preheat the oven's broiler. Line a baking sheet with aluminium foil and grease with vegetable oil.

Step 4

Arrange chicken thighs on prepared baking sheet. Discard excess marinade.

Step 5

Broil chicken in the preheated oven for 5 minutes. Flip thighs and broil for 5 minutes more. Flip thighs again and brush each thigh with reserved marinade. Continue to broil, brushing with reserved marinade every 1 to 2 minutes, until cooked through, about 12 minutes approx. Add an instant-read thermometer into the center and it should read at least 165 degrees F (74 degrees C). Garnish with dark green parts of green onions.
